# Karen M. Douglas

Karen M. Douglas is a Professor of Social Psychology at the University of Kent. Her research focuses extensively on the psychological motives behind conspiracy beliefs and the consequences of these beliefs on society.

### Key Roles & Projects
* **Conspiracy FX (effects)**: Directed this five-year, ERC-funded project (2022-on) which investigated the consequences of conspiracy theories.
* **Meta-Analytic Research**: Contributed to high-level systematic reviews regarding the motives associated with conspiracy beliefs.

### Selected Bibliography
* **What are conspiracy theories? A definitional approach to their correlates, consequences, and communication.** *Annual Review of Psychology* (2023).
* **Reasons to believe: A systematic review and meta-analytic synthesis of the motives associated with conspiracy beliefs.** *Psychological Bulletin* (2025).
